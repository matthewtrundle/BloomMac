The Science of Intrusive Thoughts

Understanding what's happening in your brain can help you feel less afraid of your thoughts.

Key Finding

Intrusive thoughts are a normal brain response to protect your baby—they don't mean you'll act on them.

Your Brain on New Motherhood

  • Amygdala hyperactivity increases threat detection
  • Prefrontal cortex changes affect emotional regulation
  • Hormonal fluctuations impact neurotransmitters

"These changes are evolutionary adaptations to help you protect your baby."

— Dr. Sarah Johnson, Perinatal Psychiatrist
