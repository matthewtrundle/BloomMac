The Spectrum of Normal

"Is this normal?" It's probably the question you ask yourself a dozen times a day.

Questioning whether your experience is normal isn't a sign of weakness. It's smart. It's protective. Today, we'll get specific about what falls within expected recovery and what needs attention.

Normal Physical Recovery

Bleeding (Lochia)

Up to 6 weeks, changing from red to pink to yellow. Clots up to golf ball size.

Night Sweats

Soaking sheets as your body dumps extra pregnancy fluid. Temporary but intense.

Hair Loss

Months 3-4, all the hair you didn't lose during pregnancy falls out. You won't go bald.

Breast Changes

Engorgement, leaking, size fluctuations. Your breasts are learning a new job.

Pelvic Changes

Leaking urine, pressure, discomfort. These muscles supported 30+ pounds.

Deep Exhaustion

Bone-deep depletion that sleep doesn't fix. Your body is in major recovery mode.

A Personal Story

My First Night Sweat

"I remember the first time waking up after having a baby, and I had soaked through not only my nightgown, but all the way to the sheets at the bottom, feeling like, is this okay?"

Let me just tell you, yes it is. It's normal for your body to release all that extra fluid. You're not sick. You're not broken. Your body is doing exactly what it needs to do.
