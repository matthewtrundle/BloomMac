The Spectrum of Normal

"Is this normal?" It's probably the question you ask yourself a dozen times a day.

Questioning whether your experience is normal isn't a sign of weakness. It's smart. It's protective. Today, we'll get specific about what falls within expected recovery and what needs attention.

Normal Physical Recovery

Bleeding (Lochia)

Up to 6 weeks, changing from red to pink to yellow. Clots up to golf ball size.

Night Sweats

Soaking sheets as your body dumps extra pregnancy fluid. Temporary but intense.

Hair Loss

Months 3-4, all the hair you didn't lose during pregnancy falls out. You won't go bald.

Breast Changes

Engorgement, leaking, size fluctuations. Your breasts are learning a new job.

Pelvic Changes

Leaking urine, pressure, discomfort. These muscles supported 30+ pounds.

Deep Exhaustion

Bone-deep depletion that sleep doesn't fix. Your body is in major recovery mode.

Normal Emotional Experiences

Baby Blues (80% of mothers)

Days 3-5 when hormones plummet. Crying, overwhelm, mood swings. Peaks at day 5, improves by 2 weeks.

Extreme Mood Swings

Intense love to feeling trapped. Rage at partner, then guilt. Your brain processing massive change.

Delayed Bonding

Not feeling "in love" immediately is common. For some it's instant, for others it grows slowly. Both normal.

Intrusive Thoughts

Scary "what if" thoughts. If you're horrified by them, that's good—they're just thoughts, not desires.

Physical Warning Signs

Heavy Bleeding

Soaking a pad in an hour or clots bigger than golf ball

Fever & Infection

Temperature over 100.4°F or foul-smelling discharge

Severe Headaches

Especially with vision changes or unresponsive to medication

Breathing Issues

Chest pain, trouble breathing, or leg pain with swelling

Don't wait. Don't minimize. Call.

Emotional Red Flags

Seek Help Immediately If:

  • ✓ Sadness or anxiety lasting more than 2 weeks without improvement
  • ✓ Can't sleep even when you have the opportunity
  • ✓ No interest in anything - not just baby, but food, shower, things you enjoyed
  • ✓ Thoughts of harming yourself or baby
  • ✓ Feeling disconnected from reality

These aren't weaknesses. They're symptoms of treatable conditions.

Taking Action

How to Get Help

Say These Exact Words:

"I'm struggling more than I think is normal. I need help figuring out what's going on."

1. Call your OB or midwife first

2. Postpartum Support International: 1-800-944-4773

3. Crisis line: 988

4. Bring someone to advocate for you

Trust Your Instincts

You know your body. You know your mind.
If something feels wrong, it probably is.

Better to be the mom who called "too much"
than the one who waited too long.

Your Tool

Daily Symptom Tracking

Track Each Day:

• Rate your mood (1-10)

• Note physical symptoms

• Track sleep quality

• Write any concerns

This data helps providers understand patterns and make accurate diagnoses.
