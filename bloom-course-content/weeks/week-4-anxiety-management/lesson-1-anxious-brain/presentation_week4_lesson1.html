When Anxiety Crosses The Line

Normal Anxiety

  • Worry that comes and goes
  • Can be distracted from it
  • Sleep when baby sleeps (mostly)
  • Function despite worry
  • Responsive to reassurance

Time for Help

  • Can't eat or sleep due to worry
  • Avoiding normal activities
  • Physical symptoms impacting health
  • Panic attacks
  • Not improving after 2-3 weeks

Seeking help isn't admitting defeat—it's choosing recovery

The Anxiety Paradox

Fighting It

💪

= More anxiety

vs

Accepting It

🌊

"Anxiety is here, that's okay"

= Less power

"I notice anxiety is here. My protective system is active. This is uncomfortable but not dangerous."

Recovery is Possible

Still having anxious thoughts but not being controlled by them

Recovery Feels Like:

Feeling worry but also feeling joy—they can coexist

Recovery Means:

Being protective without being paralyzed

Recovery Includes:

Trusting yourself even when uncertain
