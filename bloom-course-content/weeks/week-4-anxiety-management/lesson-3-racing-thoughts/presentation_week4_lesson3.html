The Thought Record

CBT simplified for exhausted mothers:

1. The thought: "I'm failing my baby"
2. The feeling: Panic, shame
3. Evidence for: Baby cried for an hour
4. Evidence against: Baby is healthy, gaining weight, mostly content
5. Balanced thought: I'm learning to understand my baby's needs

Powerful CBT Tools

Worst/Best/Most Likely

Worst: "Baby will never sleep"
Best: "Sleeps through tonight"
Most likely: "Sleep gradually improves"

Focus on probability, not catastrophe

The "So What?" Technique

"Baby isn't napping" → So what?
"They'll be cranky" → So what?
"Bedtime will be harder" → So what?
"I'll manage, like I did today"

Follow worry to manageable conclusion

The Friend Perspective

"What would I tell my best friend if she had this thought?"

We're kinder to others than ourselves

3-Question Filter

Before engaging with a thought:
1. Is this true?
2. Is this helpful?
3. Is this urgent?

Not yes to all three? Let it pass.

The Worry Window

Contain worry instead of suppressing it all day

☀️

During the Day

Write worries on list: "I'll think about this during worry time"

⏰

Worry Time

15-20 minutes daily. Review list, problem-solve what you can

😌

Results

35% anxiety reduction in 2 weeks. Brain learns worries will be addressed

Mindfulness: The Cloud Meditation

You are the sky

Vast • Unchanging • Peaceful

🍃 The River Meditation

Sit by river. Each thought is a leaf floating by. Notice it, name it ("worry leaf"), let it float downstream. Don't jump in to chase it.

📝 5-Minute Mind Dump

Write every thought for 5 minutes, no editing. Don't stop writing. When timer goes off, close notebook. Your thoughts have been heard.

Creating Thought Boundaries

Set boundaries with thoughts like you do with people:

Is this TRUE?

Based on facts, not fears

Is this HELPFUL?

Does it serve you right now?

Is this URGENT?

Needs attention today?

The Container Technique

Before Sleep:

  • Imagine a strong container
  • Place all worries inside
  • Lock it securely
  • Place outside your bedroom
Worries Safe Until Tomorrow

Tell Yourself:

"These will be here tomorrow if I need them. Tonight, I rest."

Your worries won't disappear, but they won't steal your sleep either

Repeat like a song, drowning out other thoughts
