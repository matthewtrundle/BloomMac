Dr. Jana Williams · Bloom Psychology

What Self-Compassion Is NOT

❌ Not Self-Pity

Self-compassion connects us to others; self-pity isolates us

❌ Not Weakness

It takes courage to face our pain with kindness

❌ Not Selfish

Caring for yourself enables you to care for others

The Evidence Is Clear

40%
lower depression in self-compassionate mothers

If your best friend called you exhausted and crying, you wouldn't say "Try harder." Yet that's exactly how we talk to ourselves.

Three Essential Components

1

Self-Kindness

vs. Self-Judgment

Instead of "I'm terrible for feeling this way"

Try: "This is really hard, and it's okay that I'm struggling"

2

Common Humanity

vs. Isolation

You're not uniquely flawed

92% of mothers doubt their abilities

3

Mindfulness

vs. Over-Identification

"I'm having the thought that I'm a bad mother"

Not: "I AM a bad mother"

Self-Compassion Is Medicine

Research shows self-compassionate mothers have:

  • ✓ Better sleep quality (same hours, better rest)
  • ✓ Stronger bonding with babies
  • ✓ Faster physical recovery
  • ✓ Lower anxiety levels
  • ✓ More patience with themselves & others

It's not just nice to have - it's essential for recovery

The Inner Critic's Lies

The Lies:

"Good mothers don't feel this way"

"You should be grateful"

"Everyone else manages better"

"Your baby deserves better"

The Truth:

Good mothers have all kinds of feelings

Gratitude and struggle coexist

Everyone struggles, most hide it

Your baby needs a human mother

A Simple Practice

The Self-Compassion Break

Place your hand on your heart. Feel the warmth. Take a breath.

1. Acknowledge:

"This is a moment of struggle"

2. Remember:

"Struggle is part of being human"

3. Offer:

"May I be kind to myself in this moment"

The Ripple Effect

When you're kind to yourself, you model emotional regulation for your baby.

When you acknowledge your humanity, you give others permission to be human too.

When you practice mindfulness, you're more present for the moments that matter.

Your baby doesn't need a perfect mother.
They need a mother who knows how to be kind to herself when things are hard.
