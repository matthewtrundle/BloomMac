The Window of Tolerance

HYPERAROUSAL

Anxiety • Panic • Rage • Racing thoughts

OPTIMAL ZONE

Calm • Capable • Connected • Clear

HYPOAROUSAL

Numb • Disconnected • Foggy • Shut down

Postpartum Reality:

Your window shrinks dramatically due to:

  • 😴 Sleep deprivation
  • 🌊 Hormonal changes
  • 😣 Physical pain
  • 😰 Constant overwhelm

Goal: Recognize when you've left your window and know how to return

Recognizing Where You Are

Signs of Hyperarousal

  • 💓 Heart racing
  • 🌀 Thoughts spiraling
  • 🏃‍♀️ Want to scream or run
  • ⚡ Can't sit still
  • 🚨 Everything feels urgent
  • 😤 Snapping at everyone
  • 💪 Muscles tight

Signs of Hypoarousal

  • 😶 Feel nothing
  • 🌫️ Moving through fog
  • 🤯 Can't think clearly
  • 👻 Want to disappear
  • 😔 Everything feels pointless
  • 🤖 Going through motions
  • 🫥 Disconnected from body

Both states are your nervous system trying to protect you.
Neither is wrong or bad.

Tools for Hyperarousal

When you're above your window - discharge energy and slow down:

❄️ Cold Exposure

  • • Hold ice cubes until they melt
  • • Cold shower for 30 seconds
  • • Face in bowl of cold water

Activates parasympathetic immediately

🏃‍♀️ Intense Movement

  • • Jump up and down 20 times
  • • Push against wall hard
  • • Squeeze stress ball
  • • Shake whole body vigorously

Discharges stress hormones

👋 Bilateral Stimulation

  • • Cross arms, tap shoulders
  • • March in place
  • • Look left and right rhythmically

Integrates brain hemispheres

🎵 Vocalization

  • • Hum loudly
  • • Sing anything
  • • Sigh audibly: "Ahhhhhh"
  • • Scream into pillow

Releases trapped energy

The TIPP Technique

From Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

T

Temperature

Cold water on face

I

Intense Exercise

Jumping jacks for 1 minute

P

Paced Breathing

Exhale longer than inhale

P

Paired Muscle Relaxation

Tense and release

Pick what works. The goal is to bring energy
down to manageable levels.

Tools for Hypoarousal

When you're below your window - gentle activation:

👃 Sensory Awakening

Strong smells: coffee, peppermint
Different textures: soft, rough
Intense tastes: lemon, ginger

🤸‍♀️ Gentle Movement

Slow stretches, shoulder rolls
Wiggle toes, gentle neck rolls
Start small - coaxing, not forcing

🫂 Connection

Call someone who gets it
Look at photos that spark joy
Hand on heart - self-connection

Riding the Emotional Waves

Emotions are waves. They rise, crest, and fall. Always.

Most emotions complete their cycle in
90 seconds to 20 minutes
IF we don't feed them with stories

When a big emotion hits:

  1. Name it: "Rage is here"
  2. Find it in your body
  3. Breathe into that space
  4. Let it move through you
  5. Trust it will pass

You're not your emotions. You're the ocean, not the wave.

The Power of Co-Regulation

You

Other

We're not meant to regulate alone.
Our nervous systems are designed to sync with others.

With Others:

• Soothing your baby
• Friend's hug while crying
• Partner's deep breaths
• Support group connection

When Alone:

• Pet's steady breathing
• Guided meditation
• Lean against a tree
• Soothing voice recording

You're not weak for needing others. You're human.
