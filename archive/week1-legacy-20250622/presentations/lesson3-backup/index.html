You Were Never Meant
To Do This Alone

In our culture of independence and self-reliance, asking for help can feel like failure. But the truth is, humans have always raised children in community.

"It takes a village—and you deserve one."

What Support Really Looks Like

Practical Support

Meals, childcare, household tasks

Emotional Support

Listening, validating, understanding

Professional Support

Therapy, medical care, expertise

What Gets in the Way

  • 🚫
    "I should be able to handle this myself"
  • 😰
    Fear of being judged or misunderstood
  • 🔄
    Not knowing what you need or how to ask
  • 💭
    Believing you're a burden to others

These barriers are real, but they don't have to be permanent.

The Village Helper

The Listener

The Professional

The Truth-Tellers

The Inner Support

Self-compassion as foundation

Steps to Building Your Support Network

1

Identify Your Needs

What would actually help you feel supported right now?

2

Map Your Current Circle

Who's already in your life? Who could you reconnect with?

3

Start Small

One text, one call, one "yes" to an offer of help

4

Join a Community

Find groups where others understand your experience

5

Be Specific

"Can you watch the baby for 30 minutes?" is easier than "I need help"

6

Receive Gracefully

Let people help without feeling guilty
